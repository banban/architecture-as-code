1. Contextual Architecture Process ()
In the SABSA Model business view is called the contextual security architecture. It is a description of the business context in which your secure system must be designed, built and operated. Example. Human Goal A. To live a collective, collaborative long life with qualities on every aspect of the mind, body and the spirit, in current and future environments through-life, to reproduce for survival of the species. On the Contextual layer, where it starts with understanding the context, we will need to identify the core life values that account for all human beings, comprising only of commonalities between different groups of people, and different individuals. These core values would go for the first requirement of the Human Architecture. Contextually Humankind is occupied on a day-to-day basis, 24 hours a day, with trying to stay alive, in order for the survival of the species, in our universe, by consuming food and water supplements, reproducing, balanced between activities and rest during night and day. Together with other humans, animals and in the climate conditions of our (geographical) surroundings. Based on this Goal and its context the next step comprises of the identification of threats and opportunities to and of this Goal in its context.
